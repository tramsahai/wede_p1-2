About Shakti Foundation

Transforming lives through sustainable empowerment since 1992

Our Story

Shakti Foundation was founded in 1992 in Dhaka, Bangladesh by Dr. Humaira Islam and a team of social innovators dedicated to empowering underprivileged women through microfinance.

What began as a pilot microfinance program for women living in urban slums rapidly expanded into both urban and rural regions, transforming Shakti into one of the largest NGOs in Bangladesh.

Today, Shakti operates across 57 districts with over 475,199 members. While microcredit remains central to our mission, we now offer comprehensive programs including healthcare, education, agro-business, renewable energy, skills training, and advocacy.

Dr. Humaira Islam's Journey

Dr. Humaira Islam, a visionary social entrepreneur, founded Shakti Foundation in 1992 with a simple yet powerful belief: that every woman deserves the opportunity to become self-reliant and empowered.

Early Life & Education: Born and raised in Dhaka, Dr. Islam witnessed firsthand the struggles women faced in accessing financial resources. She pursued degrees in Social Work and Economics, specializing in microfinance and women's economic empowerment.

The Beginning: In 1992, with a small team of like-minded individuals, she started a pilot microfinance program in the urban slums of Dhaka. The program provided small loans to women entrepreneurs who were excluded from traditional banking systems.

Growth & Impact: Under her leadership, Shakti grew from serving 50 women in 1992 to over 475,000 members today. She pioneered innovative approaches that combined microfinance with education, healthcare, and skills training.

Recognition: Dr. Islam has received numerous awards for her work, including the National Social Service Award and recognition from international development organizations.

Philosophy: "Empower a woman, and you empower an entire community. When women have access to resources and opportunities, they become catalysts for sustainable change."

Our Vision

To empower disadvantaged women to become leaders, entrepreneurs, and agents of change in their communities, creating a world where gender equality is a reality.

Our Mission

To strengthen the social and economic foundations of poor women in Bangladesh through microfinance, education, healthcare, and skills training, while encouraging donations and partnerships to sustain these efforts.
